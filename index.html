FAQ

Common questions

Do I need an appointment for an oil change or state inspection?

No — walk-ins are welcome any business day. State inspections are typically done in 15–20 minutes. For brake or alignment work, we recommend calling ahead so we can have the bay ready.

How much does a set of 4 new tires cost?

It depends on size and brand. Most passenger cars run $400–$800 for 4 quality tires installed (mounting, balancing, valve stems and disposal included). Trucks and SUVs run higher. Call us with your tire size (it's on the sidewall, like 225/65R17) and we'll quote you in 5 minutes.

Do you honor my extended warranty?

Yes — we work with most major extended warranty companies (Endurance, CarShield, dealer warranties, etc.). Bring your contract or warranty card and we'll handle the claim paperwork for you.

What's covered by your repair warranty?

Most repairs are backed by a 24-month / 24,000-mile warranty on parts and labor — whichever comes first. If a covered part fails in that window, we replace it free, including labor.

Can you do a pre-purchase inspection on a used car?

Yes. For $89 we'll do a full mechanical inspection on a used car you're thinking of buying — engine, transmission, brakes, suspension, frame, tires, fluid condition. You get a written report. Cheap insurance against a $5,000 surprise.

Do you offer financing?

Yes. We offer 6-month interest-free financing on repairs over $250 through Synchrony Car Care, plus 90-day no-credit-needed options for tires through Snap Finance and Acima.
